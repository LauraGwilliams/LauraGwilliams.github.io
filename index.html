A Short Introduction

I am currently a junior researcher in the Neuroscience of Language Lab at New York University Abu Dhabi, working under the supervision of Alec Marantz and Liina Pylkk√§nen. In 2013 I graduated from the Cognitive Neuroscience of Language Masters degree programme, taught by the Basque Centre on Cognition, Brain and Language (BCBL) in the Basque Country. Prior to this, I graduated from Cardiff University in 2012 with a first class (hons) degree in Linguistics.

My research is focused within the neurobiology of language, borne from my interests in theoretical linguistics, psychology and cognitive neuroscience. I am specifically interested in exploring the answers to questions which help us understand the structual representation of words in the mental lexicon. I am currently conducting research across both Romance and Semitic languages, using a combination of behavioural and neuroimaging techniques, with a focus on auditory processing.

In addition, I am also very interested in how lexical and sub-lexical contextual information is integrated in order to modify predictions of the incoming speech signal, and I hope to investigate this topic in the future.
