A Short Introduction

I am currently a first year graduate student in the Cognition and Perception programme at NYU, co-supervised by Alec Marantz and David Poeppel.

Broadly speaking, my research attempts to bring together knowledge from theoretical linguistics, psychology and cognitive neuroscience in order to gain a comprehensive understanding of how the brain processes language and all its peculiarities. In specific terms, I work on understanding the connection between speech processing and access to stored representations, be it the phoneme, morpheme or lexical item.

I have been, and continue to be, lucky enough to have access to experimental technologies that are particularly suited for the study of auditory processing, such as MEG and EEG. A lot of my work therefore adopts these methodologies, which allow for precise temporal insight into the neural mechanisms that support language comprehension.
